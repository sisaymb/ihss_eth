  1. Definition of terms

Various measures of central tendency and dispersion were used in the Delphi study to understand the “average” score and “expert consensus / dispersion” in the scores. Below are the various measures provided in the various tables, charts

Measures of central tendency
  • Mean: It is the average of the scores provided and computed as the sum of the scores divided by the sample size. Since the man is affected by outliers, we will use median as a key measure of central tendencey in this analysis
  • Median: It is the value that equally divides the expert opinions. 50% of the values are below and above the score. The median is robust to outliers and is used as measure of central tendency
Measures of dispersion / consensus
  • Standard deviation: It is among the common measures of dispersion in data which is computed as \(\sigma = \sqrt{\frac{1}{N} \sum_{i=1}^{N-1} (x_i - \mu)^2}\) whre x is the criterion or intervention being analyzed and N is the sample size. Since the \(\sigma\) is sensitive to outliers it is not used in assessing dispersion in this study.
  • Interquartile range (IQR): It is a measure reperesnting the range between the first quartile (p25) and third quartile (p75) and shows the spread of the middle 50% of the data. It is computed as \(IQR = Q3 - Q1\) where Q3 and Q1 are the 75% (i.e. 75% of the observations are below Q3) and 25% of the data (25% of obs below Q1). IQR is used as primary measure of displersion in the study The cutoffs are:
    • Cutoffs for criterion weight: IQR < 5 : Stable (consensus among experts), 5 > IQR < 10: Slightly unstable (minor difference among experts), IQR > 10: Unstable (significant difference among experts)
    • Cutoffs for intervention scores (IQR is expected to be bn 1 and 4): IQR < 1: Stable (consensus among experts), 1 > IQR < 2: Slightly unstable (minor difference among experts), ** IQR > 2: Unstable** (significant difference among experts)
  • Rescaled median absolute deviation (RMAD): It is a rescaled (0 to 1) of the median absolute deviation which is relatively more robust to outliers and data scale thatn IQR. IT is computed as \(RMAD = median(|x_i - median(x_i)|) / ((b - a)/2)\) where a, b are the theoretical min and max of the variables. For criterion weights the min and max are assumed to be 5 and 35 while for intervetnion weighting the min and max are 1 and 5 respectively. The RMAD will be used as secondary measure of dispersion with the following cutoffs: RMAD < 0.25 : Stable (consensus among experts), 0.25 > RMAD < 0.5: Slightly unstable (minor difference among experts), RMAD > 0.5: Unstable (significant difference among experts)
